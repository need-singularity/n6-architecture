# Sleep Medicine (HEXA-SLEEPMED) — n=6 AASM sleep staging

## §1 WHY

**AASM sleep staging** classifies sleep into **6 stages**: W (wake), N1, N2,
N3 (slow-wave sleep), REM, plus the NREM-total summary arc. Standard sleep
cycles run ~90 min and a full-night (~9 h) cycles ≈ 6 times — σ(6)/2 = 6.
Polysomnography routinely uses the σ(6)=12 channel EEG 10-20 convention.

## §2 n=6 CONNECTION

- **AASM 6-stage staging** — the clinical diagnostic primitive.
- **~6 sleep cycles per full night** (~90 min per cycle).
- **PSG 12-channel EEG** = σ(6) = 12.

## §3 DIFFERENTIATION

Distinct from `neuro`, `neuroscience` (cognitive / structural brain science).
Sleep medicine is the device-focused clinical discipline (PSG, CPAP, actigraphy).

## §4 SOURCES

- AASM Sleep Staging Manual (latest revision).
- Buysse et al. 1989, *Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index*.
- PSG EEG 10-20 montage standard.
