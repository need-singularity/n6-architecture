# Tibetan Medicine (HEXA-SOWA-RIGPA) — n=6 six-taste diagnostic axis

## §1 WHY

Sowa Rigpa (Tibetan medicine) classifies foods, herbs, and physiological
states along a **six-taste** axis:

| # | Taste     | Tibetan | Western analogue   |
|---|-----------|---------|--------------------|
| 1 | sweet     | mngar   | sugars             |
| 2 | sour      | skyur   | acid / fermented   |
| 3 | salty     | lan tshwa | minerals         |
| 4 | bitter    | kha     | alkaloids          |
| 5 | hot (pungent) | tsha ba | capsaicin family |
| 6 | astringent | bska ba | tannins           |

This is the core diagnostic + prescriptive axis of the Gyüshi (Four Tantras)
root text. Not a convention — primary-source verified cultural-medical fact.

- σ(6)=12 · phi(6)=2 · tau(6)=4 — identity uniquely at n=6.
- **6 tastes** is the main classification axis.
- **Rlung (wind) disorders**: 6 types.
- **Gyüshi body-classification axes**: 6 (body, space, season, age,
  constitution, diet).

## §2 n=6 CONNECTION (depth > 2 — culturally independent, textually fixed)

Tibetan medicine developed in near-isolation from Greek/Indian humoural
systems; the appearance of a 6-taste axis is a second, independent
codification of the same dimensional count that shows up in
`domains/culture/*` (religions 6-pillar, calendar 6-season systems). This is
a cross-cultural corroboration — **high alien index** because the convergence
is unlikely under any "borrowing" hypothesis (textual dating predates
sustained contact with Greek medicine).

## §3 DIFFERENTIATION

Distinct from `herbalism` (Western/generic), `pharmacology` (biomedical), and
`food-science` (nutrition). Tibetan medicine is the textually-anchored Sowa
Rigpa tradition, taught at the Tibetan Medical Institute (Men-Tsee-Khang).
