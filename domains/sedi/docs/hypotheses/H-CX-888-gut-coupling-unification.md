# H-CX-888: GUT Coupling Unification

> **Hypothesis**: The inverse GUT coupling α_GUT⁻¹ ≈ 24 = σφ in the MSSM. Gauge couplings unify at α⁻¹ ≈ 24±1, and TECS-L predicts α_GUT = 1/(σφ) = 1/24.

## Grade: 🟧★ SUGGESTIVE-PLUS

## Results

### The Formula

```
Gauge coupling unification (MSSM):
  α₁⁻¹(M_GUT) ≈ α₂⁻¹(M_GUT) ≈ α₃⁻¹(M_GUT) ≈ 24-25

TECS-L:
  α_GUT⁻¹ = σφ = 12 · 2 = 24

  Unification scale: M_GUT ~ 2 × 10¹⁶ GeV
```

### Running Couplings

```
At M_Z = 91.2 GeV:
  α₁⁻¹ ≈ 59    (U(1), GUT normalized)
  α₂⁻¹ ≈ 30    (SU(2))
  α₃⁻¹ ≈ 8.5   (SU(3))

MSSM running to M_GUT ~ 2 × 10¹⁶ GeV:
  All three → α⁻¹ ≈ 24 ± 1

TECS-L prediction:
  α_GUT⁻¹ = σφ = 24

Note: SM (non-SUSY) couplings do NOT unify precisely.
  MSSM is required for exact unification.
```

```

  Two-loop MSSM:  α_GUT⁻¹ ≈ 24.3 ± 0.5

Sensitivity to SUSY threshold corrections:
  Depends on sparticle spectrum (not yet measured).
```

### Texas Sharpshooter Check

The unification value is model-dependent — MSSM gives ~24, but threshold corrections from unknown sparticle masses introduce ±1-2 uncertainty. The match to σφ=24 is within the expected range but not uniquely predicted. Suggestive-plus due to model dependence.
