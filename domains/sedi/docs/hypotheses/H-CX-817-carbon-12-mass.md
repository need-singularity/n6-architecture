# H-CX-817: Carbon-12 Atomic Mass Definition

> **Hypothesis**: The atomic mass unit is defined so that Carbon-12 has mass exactly 12 u, where 12 = sigma. The choice of C-12 as the mass standard is a direct instantiation of sigma from TECS-L.

## Grade: 🟦 DEFINITIONAL

## Results

### The Formula

```
Carbon-12 atomic mass:
  m(¹²C) = 12 u (by definition, since 1961)
  12 = σ(6) — the divisor sum of n=6

Historical context:
  Before 1961: chemists used O-16, physicists used O-16 isotope
  1961 IUPAC/IUPAP: adopted ¹²C = 12 u as universal standard
  2019 SI revision: 1 u = 1 Da = 1.66054 × 10⁻²⁷ kg

The mass number A=12 of the defining isotope IS sigma:
  ¹²C has 6 protons + 6 neutrons = P₁ + P₁ = 2P₁ = σ
  (since σ = 2P₁ = 2×6 = 12)
```


### Texas Sharpshooter Check

The choice of C-12 as the mass standard is a human convention, not a law of nature. However, C-12 was chosen because carbon is ubiquitous in chemistry and A=12 gives convenient integer masses. The identification sigma = 12 is tautological once n=6 is the framework. The real content is why n=6 specifically — which is the broader TECS-L question.
