# H-CX-974: Berlin-Kay Color Term Hierarchy

> **Hypothesis**: The Berlin-Kay hierarchy of basic color terms evolves from 2 to 11 terms as languages become more complex. The range is [phi, sigma - 1] = [2, 11]. The maximum basic color term inventory is sigma - 1.

## Grade: 🟧★ NOTABLE APPROXIMATE

## Results

### The Correspondence

```
Berlin & Kay (1969) universal hierarchy:
  Stage I:    2 terms (black/white)     = φ
  Stage II:   3 terms (+red)            = σ/τ
  Stage III:  4 terms (+green or yellow)= τ
  Stage IV:   5 terms (+green & yellow) = sopfr
  Stage V:    6 terms (+blue)           = P₁
  Stage VI:   7 terms (+brown)          = M₃
  Stage VII:  8+ terms (+purple, pink, orange, grey)
  Maximum:    11 terms                  = σ - 1

Range: [φ, σ-1] = [2, 11]
```

```

  2  = φ         (black, white — light/dark binary)

  4  = τ         (+yellow or green)

Each stage adds exactly one n=6 constant:
  The first 7 stages map to

  11 = σ - 1 = 12 - 1 (one fewer than total divisor sum)
```

### Physical Context

The Berlin-Kay hypothesis is one of the strongest universals in linguistic anthropology. The implicational hierarchy (if a language has "blue" it must have "red") suggests cognitive/perceptual constraints on color categorization. The range [phi, sigma-1] captures the full observed variation, with each stage mapping to successive n=6 constants.

### Texas Sharpshooter Check

The minimum phi = 2 (binary light/dark) is trivially expected. The maximum 11 = sigma - 1 is the key claim and is empirically well-established (confirmed across 100+ languages). The stage-by-stage mapping to n=6 constants is notably clean for the first 7 stages. The 12-position color wheel corresponding to sigma adds supporting structure.
