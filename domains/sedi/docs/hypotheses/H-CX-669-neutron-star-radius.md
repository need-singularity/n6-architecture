# H-CX-669: Neutron Star Radius R_NS ≈ σ(6) = 12 km

> **Hypothesis**: The typical neutron star radius R_NS ≈ 12 km = σ(6) km, the divisor sum of the first perfect number.

## Grade: 🟧★ (within 1σ of NICER measurements)

## Results

### The Prediction

```
R_NS = σ(6) km = 12 km

NICER measurements (Riley et al. 2021):
  R_1.4 = 12.45 ± 0.65 km  (for 1.4 M_☉ NS)

Error: (12.45 - 12)/12.45 = 3.6%, well within 1σ
```

### Why σ(6)?

```
σ(6) = 1 + 2 + 3 + 6 = 12

The divisor sum encodes the complete factorization structure of n=6.
A neutron star of 1.4 M_☉ (= M_Ch, see H-CX-663) has radius σ(6) km.
```

### Nuclear Physics Connection

The neutron star radius is determined by the nuclear equation of state (EOS). The fact that R ≈ 12 km emerges from nuclear saturation density n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ and symmetry energy. That this value coincides with σ(6) suggests the nuclear force scale itself carries n=6 structure.

### EOS Constraint

```
R_1.4 = 12 km implies a moderately stiff EOS
Tidal deformability: Λ_1.4 ≈ 400-600 (GW170817 constraint)
```
