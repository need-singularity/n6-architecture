# H-CX-1055: Primordial Black Holes as Dark Matter

> **Hypothesis**: If primordial black holes (PBHs) constitute all dark matter, the required mass is M_PBH ~ 10^20 g. The exponent 20 = C(6,3) = sopfr * tau, the binomial coefficient of n=6 choose sigma/tau, and equivalently the product of prime-factor sum and divisor count.

### Physical Context

Primordial black holes could form from large density fluctuations during inflation or phase transitions. The asteroid-mass window (10^17-10^23 g) remains the only mass range where PBHs could constitute all dark matter, as lighter PBHs would have evaporated via Hawking radiation and heavier ones are constrained by microlensing surveys. Future missions (e.g., femtolensing surveys, asteroid-ranging experiments) may probe this window.

### Texas Sharpshooter Check

The exponent 20 is the center of a broad allowed range (17-23 in grams). The match to C(6,3) = 20 is exact and structurally meaningful (maximal binomial coefficient of n=6). The Schwarzschild radius exponent -8 = -(sigma-tau) adds coherence. However, the broad mass window means several exponents would be "in range." Grade reflects the clean binomial structure and multi-exponent consistency.
