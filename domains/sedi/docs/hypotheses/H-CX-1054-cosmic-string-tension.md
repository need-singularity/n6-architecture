### Physical Context

Cosmic strings are one-dimensional topological defects that could form during phase transitions in the early universe. They are also predicted in string theory as fundamental or D-strings stretched to cosmological scales. Current observations constrain but do not exclude cosmic strings. The CMB bound from Planck (G*mu < 10^-7) is the most model-independent constraint. If detected, cosmic strings would provide direct evidence for high-energy symmetry breaking.

### Texas Sharpshooter Check

The exponent -7 is the bound's order of magnitude, which is a single integer. Matching it to M_3 = 7 is neat but the space of TECS-L values near 7 is dense (P_1=6, M_3=7, sigma-tau=8). The bound is also model-dependent. The pulsar timing bound (-11) matching sigma-1 adds a second data point. Grade reflects exact exponent matches tempered by the simplicity of matching single integers.
