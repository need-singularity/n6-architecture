### Physical Context

The cosmological lithium problem is one of the most persistent anomalies in Big Bang nucleosynthesis. While BBN accurately predicts deuterium and helium-4 abundances to within a few percent, the predicted primordial lithium-7 abundance exceeds observations by a factor of approximately 3. Proposed solutions include stellar depletion mechanisms, non-standard BBN physics, and new particle physics, but none has been universally accepted. The coincidence that lithium (Z=3) is depleted by a factor of 3 adds a numerological layer.

### Texas Sharpshooter Check

The match to tau/sigma = 1/3 is compelling because 3 is also the atomic number of lithium and the nucleon count 7 = M_3. However, factor-of-3 ratios are common enough to appear by coincidence. The elevated grade reflects the triple coincidence (Z=3, factor~3, M_3 nucleons).
