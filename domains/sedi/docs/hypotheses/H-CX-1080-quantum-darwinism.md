### Physical Context

Quantum Darwinism, developed by Wojciech Zurek, explains how classical objectivity emerges from quantum mechanics. The environment acts as a witness: when many fragments of the environment each carry a copy of the system's state, that state becomes objective — observable by many without disturbance. The redundancy R_D counts how many independent copies exist. When R_D = 1, information is quantum (fragile); when R_D >> 1, it is classical (robust). The quantum-classical transition occurs as R_D crosses unity — exactly R(6)=1.

### Texas Sharpshooter Check

The threshold R_D = 1 is natural in any redundancy measure (one copy is the minimum for any information to exist). Identifying this with R(6)=1 is technically exact but conceptually broad. The deeper point is that R(6)=1 represents a universal balance point, and the quantum-classical transition is another instance of crossing that balance.
