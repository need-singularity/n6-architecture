## Results

### Classical Doppler

```
Sound (non-relativistic):
  f' = f · (v ± v_obs) / (v ∓ v_src)

Structure: ratio of sums/differences
  Approaching: f' = f · (v + v_obs)/(v - v_src)  → blueshift
  Receding:    f' = f · (v - v_obs)/(v + v_src)  → redshift

Number of sign combinations: 4 = τ
  (both approaching, both receding, mixed × 2)
```

### Relativistic Doppler

```
For light (special relativity):
  f' = f · √[(1+β)/(1-β)]    where β = v/c

Key structural element: the square root → power 1/φ
  f'/f = [(1+β)/(1-β)]^(1/φ)

At β = 0: f'/f = 1 = R(6)        (no shift)
At β → 1: f'/f → ∞               (infinite blueshift)
```

### Physical Context

The Doppler effect describes frequency shifts due to relative motion between source and observer. For sound, the medium provides a preferred frame, giving asymmetric formulas. For light, Lorentz invariance demands the symmetric relativistic form with its characteristic square root.
