### Physical Context

Thermal leptogenesis, proposed by Fukugita and Yanagida, generates the observed matter-antimatter asymmetry through CP-violating decays of heavy right-handed neutrinos. The Davidson-Ibarra bound sets the minimum mass of the lightest right-handed neutrino at ~10⁹ GeV, which in turn requires the universe to reheat to at least this temperature after inflation. This energy scale sits between the electroweak and GUT scales, and the exponent 9 = 3² emerges from the seesaw mechanism's quadratic dependence on Yukawa couplings.

### Texas Sharpshooter Check

The exponent 9 is a well-established lower bound from the seesaw mechanism. Writing 9 = 3² = (σ/τ)² involves choosing one specific factorization. However, the baryon asymmetry η_B ~ 6×10⁻¹⁰ independently matching P₁·10^(-τ(P₃)) strengthens the case.
