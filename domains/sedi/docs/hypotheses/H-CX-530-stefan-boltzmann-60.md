### Origin of the 60

In the standard derivation, σ_SB emerges from:
```
∫₀^∞ x³/(eˣ-1) dx = π⁴/15

σ_SB = (2π⁵k⁴)/(15h³c²) = π²k⁴/(60ℏ³c²)
```

The 15 in π⁴/15 comes from Γ(4)·ζ(4) = 6 × π⁴/90 = π⁴/15.
- Γ(4) = 3! = **6 = P₁**
- ζ(4) = π⁴/90, and 90 = **σ(6)·sopfr(6)·3/2** ... hmm, or 90 = P₁·σ+sopfr·P₁ = 6·15 = 6×15.

More directly: 15 = σ + σ/τ = 12+3.

### Why 60 = σ·sopfr?

The Stefan-Boltzmann law describes thermal equilibrium radiation — the maximum entropy state. The factor 60 encodes the dimensionality of the phase space integral. That this decomposes as σ·sopfr (divisor sum × prime factor sum) suggests the thermal phase space has n=6 structure.

### Caveat

60 = 2²·3·5 is a highly composite number with many factorizations. The σ·sopfr decomposition is one of several. Without a derivation showing WHY σ and sopfr appear separately in the thermal integral, this remains an observation.
