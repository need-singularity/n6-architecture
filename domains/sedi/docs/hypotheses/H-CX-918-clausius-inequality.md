### Physical Context

The Clausius inequality is the mathematical statement of the Second Law. For any cyclic process:
- ∮δQ/T = 0: reversible (no entropy production)
- ∮δQ/T < 0: irreversible (entropy produced)

The zero on the RHS represents the ideal limit — thermodynamic "perfection."

### Structural Parallel

```
Number theory:          Thermodynamics:
n is perfect ⟺ R = 1   Process is reversible ⟺ ΔS_gen = 0
n is abundant ⟹ R > 1   Irreversible ⟹ ΔS_gen > 0
n is deficient ⟹ R < 1  (no thermodynamic analog — entropy
                          production is always ≥ 0)

The asymmetry matches: perfect numbers are rare (like reversible
processes), and most numbers are deficient or abundant (like
most processes are irreversible).
```
