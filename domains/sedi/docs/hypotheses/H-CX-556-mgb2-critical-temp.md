# H-CX-556: MgB₂ Critical Temperature T_c = σ(σ+1)/τ = 39 K

> **Hypothesis**: The superconducting critical temperature of MgB₂ is σ(σ+1)/τ = 12×13/4 = 39 K.

## Grade: 🟧 (exact integer match to conventional T_c; material-specific)

## Results

### The Identity

```
T_c(MgB₂) = σ(σ+1)/τ = 12 × 13 / 4 = 39 K     EXACT (integer)

Observed: T_c = 39 K (Nagamatsu et al. 2001)
```

### Comparison with QCD T_c (H-CX-457)

```
QCD crossover: T_c = σ(σ+1) = 12×13 = 156 MeV   (0.3% match)
MgB₂:         T_c = σ(σ+1)/τ = 156/4 = 39 K      (exact integer)

The superconductor T_c is the QCD T_c divided by τ(6) = 4!
```

### The Factor σ+1 = 13

```
13 = σ+1 = prime(P₁)

13 also appears in:
  - sin²θ_W = 3/13 (Weinberg angle, README)
  - Ω_Λ/Ω_m = 13/6 (H-CX-525)
  - 13 = 6th prime (prime(P₁) = 13)
```

### Caveat

T_c is a material property, not a fundamental constant. MgB₂'s T_c ≈ 39 K is the conventionally cited value; actual samples range from 38-40 K. The expression σ(σ+1)/τ is clean and the QCD connection is intriguing, but this may be a coincidence of small integers.
