### Physical Context

The stability of the electroweak vacuum depends on the Higgs self-coupling running to high energies. With the measured Higgs mass of 125 GeV and top mass of ~173 GeV, the coupling turns negative around 10¹⁰-10¹² GeV, rendering the vacuum metastable. The critical Higgs mass for absolute stability is ~129-130 GeV. That the Higgs mass falls just below this threshold — in the narrow metastable window — is one of the most striking features of the measured Standard Model parameters.
