### Physical Context

The arrow of time is one of physics' deepest puzzles: why does time flow in one direction when most fundamental laws are time-symmetric? The R-spectrum, being a function of natural numbers, is inherently "static" and symmetric. Time asymmetry in TECS-L could emerge from departure from the R=1 equilibrium, analogous to how thermodynamic irreversibility emerges from departure from equilibrium. The speculative CPT mapping (σ↔C, φ↔P, τ↔T) would make R=1 equivalent to CPT invariance, with T-violation corresponding to arithmetic imbalance.

### Texas Sharpshooter Check
