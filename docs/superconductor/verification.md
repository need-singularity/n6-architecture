Grading scale:
- **EXACT**: The claimed number/structure matches real-world data precisely, with a legitimate physical basis.
- **CLOSE**: Within ~10% of real values, or directionally correct with a physically meaningful classification.
- **WEAK**: Requires cherry-picking, flexible counting, or post-hoc rationalization.
- **FAIL**: Contradicted by real-world data, trivially true of any number, or unit-dependent.
- **UNVERIFIABLE**: Insufficient published data to confirm or deny.

## H-SC-1: Cooper Pair = phi(6)=2 Electrons

**Grade: CLOSE** (upgraded from original CLOSE -- confirmed)

Cooper pairs consist of exactly 2 electrons bound via phonon-mediated attraction. This is the cornerstone of BCS theory (Bardeen, Cooper, Schrieffer, 1957). The number 2 is physically forced: pairing two spin-1/2 fermions produces a spin-0 boson, the minimum requirement for Bose-Einstein condensation. Three-body bound states (trions) exist in other contexts but are energetically unfavorable for electrons in a Fermi sea.

The match phi(6)=2 is real, but "2" is the most common small integer in physics. The same number arises from dozens of unrelated sources. Nevertheless, Cooper pairing is genuinely the defining structural unit of superconductivity, and it genuinely equals phi(6). The original CLOSE grade is fair.

## H-SC-2: BCS Gap Ratio 3.528 from n=6

**Grade: FAIL** (confirmed)

The BCS weak-coupling gap ratio 2Delta(0)/k_B T_c = 2*pi/e^gamma = 3.5278... where gamma = 0.5772 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant. This is an exact analytic result from the BCS gap equation. The value derives entirely from pi and gamma -- fundamental mathematical constants with no connection to number-theoretic functions of 6. The attempted match sigma(6)/tau(6) + mu(6)/phi(6) = 3 + 0.5 = 3.5 is 0.8% off and requires combining four different arithmetic functions in an arbitrary way. Any target number can be approximated this way with enough functions.

## H-SC-3: BCS Coherence Length from n=6

**Grade: FAIL** (confirmed)

The BCS coherence length xi_0 = hbar*v_F/(pi*Delta(0)) depends on the Fermi velocity v_F and the gap Delta(0), both material-specific quantities. Values range from ~1.5 nm (YBCO ab-plane) to ~1600 nm (Al). There is no universal numerical constant here that could match n=6 arithmetic. The hypothesis correctly identifies this and assigns FAIL.

## H-SC-4: London Penetration Depth from n=6

**Grade: FAIL** (confirmed)

London penetration depth lambda_L = sqrt(m/(mu_0 * n_s * e^2)) depends on superfluid density n_s, which is entirely material-specific. Values: Al ~50 nm, Nb ~39 nm, YBCO ~150 nm. The hypothesis attempts to connect 1/sqrt(2) = 0.707 (the GL kappa threshold) to mu(6)/sqrt(phi(6)), which is trivially 1/sqrt(2) -- this is circular, not insightful. No universal n=6 pattern exists.

## H-SC-5: Phase Transition Orders = Divisors of 6

**Grade: FAIL** (confirmed)

First-order and second-order phase transitions are universal Ehrenfest classifications applicable to all phase transitions in nature (liquid-gas, magnetic, structural, etc.). The set {1, 2} is a subset of the divisors of 6, but it is also a subset of the divisors of every even number. This has zero specificity to n=6.

## H-SC-6: Isotope Effect Exponent alpha=1/2

**Grade: FAIL** (confirmed)

The BCS isotope exponent alpha = 1/2 follows directly from the Debye frequency omega_D proportional to M^(-1/2), which is the standard harmonic oscillator result omega = sqrt(k/m). This is freshman physics. The exponent 1/2 appears in every mass-spring system in nature. Mapping it to mu(6)/phi(6) = 1/2 adds nothing. Moreover, experimental deviations from alpha=0.5 are common: Pb has alpha=0.49, but many unconventional superconductors have alpha near 0 or even negative.

## H-SC-7: Froehlich Hamiltonian 3 Terms

**Grade: WEAK** (confirmed)

The electron-phonon Hamiltonian H = H_el + H_ph + H_el-ph has three terms, matching n/phi(6)=3. However, ANY interacting quantum system decomposes as H = H_system1 + H_system2 + H_interaction. This is true for QED (electron + photon + coupling), QCD, nuclear forces, etc. The three-term structure reflects the universal pattern of "two subsystems plus their interaction," not anything specific to superconductivity or n=6.

## H-SC-8: BEC-BCS Crossover 3 Regimes

**Grade: WEAK** (confirmed)

The BEC-BCS crossover is parameterized by 1/(k_F*a), where a is the scattering length. The three regimes (BCS: a<0, unitary: a=infinity, BEC: a>0) represent the natural division of a real-valued parameter into negative/zero/positive. Any continuous parameter produces this same trichotomy. The "3" here is not specific to superconductivity.

**Grade: FAIL** (confirmed)

The magnetic susceptibility chi = -1 for a perfect diamagnet is the DEFINITION of complete flux expulsion, not a derived quantity. Saying |chi|=1=mu(6) is equivalent to saying "complete = 1," which is a tautology. Every "complete" or "perfect" physical effect has a parameter equal to 1 by definition.

This is the universal structure of ANY U(1) symmetry breaking: superfluids, BEC, XY magnets, etc. The number 2 here is the dimensionality of the complex plane, which is a mathematical universal. Mapping it to phi(6)=2 provides no insight.

The original CLOSE is too generous for what amounts to "a threshold divides things into 2."

## H-SC-12: Type II Has phi(6)=2 Critical Fields

**Grade: FAIL** (downgraded from WEAK)

Hc1 and Hc2 are the lower and upper critical fields of Type II superconductors. Having 2 boundaries is inherent in the definition of a "mixed state" -- you need an entry point and an exit point, so there must be exactly 2. If you include the surface critical field Hc3 = 1.695*Hc2 (Saint-James & de Gennes), there are 3. The number 2 is definitionally forced and trivial.

NbTi has Tc ~ 9.2-10 K depending on composition, which can be loosely matched to sigma(6)-phi(6)=10 for one composition. The match Nb3Sn Tc ~ 3n is genuinely interesting as a numerical coincidence but has no causal mechanism. The Nb Tc = 9.3 ~ sigma-n/phi = 9 is 3.2% off -- borderline.

## H-SC-16: GL kappa Threshold 1/sqrt(2)

Mapping sqrt(2) to sqrt(phi(6)) is just restating that phi(6)=2, which adds nothing.

## H-SC-19: Abrikosov Vortex Lattice Coordination = 6

**Grade: EXACT** (confirmed)

Abrikosov vortices in Type II superconductors (Hc1 < H < Hc2) form a triangular (hexagonal) lattice where each vortex has exactly 6 nearest neighbors. This was predicted by Abrikosov (1957) from GL theory and confirmed by decoration experiments (Essmann & Trauble, 1967) and neutron scattering. The triangular lattice is the energy minimum, ~2% lower than square. The coordination number 6 is a mathematical consequence of 2D close-packing (the kissing number in 2D is 6). This is the same geometric inevitability as honeycombs and snowflakes. While n=6 does not "cause" this, the match is exact and physically robust. This is the strongest hypothesis in the set.

## H-SC-20: Single Vortex = mu(6)=1 Quantum

**Grade: FAIL** (confirmed)

Each Abrikosov vortex carries exactly one flux quantum Phi_0. This is because multi-quantum vortices (n>1) are topologically unstable and decompose into n single-quantum vortices (energetically favorable since E ~ n^2 for multi-quantum vs n*E_1 for separated). The number 1 is the minimum winding number, a topological invariant. Mapping "1" to mu(6)=1 is trivial -- every minimum or unit quantity equals 1.

The number of phases ranges from 3 to 6+ depending on the material and the level of detail. The count of "4" requires specific boundary choices.

## H-SC-23: M-H Curve Regions = tau(6)=4

Adding hysteresis effects to reach 4 is mixing equilibrium and non-equilibrium descriptions. This is post-hoc adjustment to reach the target number.

## H-SC-24: YBCO Metal Atoms 1:2:3, Sum=6

**Grade: EXACT** (confirmed)

YBa2Cu3O7-delta has the metal atom ratio Y:Ba:Cu = 1:2:3. The sum 1+2+3 = 6, and the set {1,2,3} is exactly the set of proper divisors of 6. This is a verifiable crystallographic fact. The 1:2:3 ratio arises from the triple-perovskite stacking sequence (Y layer + 2 BaO layers + 3 Cu-O layers), which is structurally determined. While n=6 does not cause this structure, the match is exact, non-trivial (the specific ratio 1:2:3 is not generic), and involves the most important HTS material. The original CLOSE grade is too conservative -- this is a precise structural match.

## H-SC-27: Optimal CuO2 Planes = 3 = n/phi

**Grade: CLOSE** (confirmed)

The experimental observation that Tc is maximized at n_L = 3 CuO2 planes per unit cell is well-established across multiple cuprate families: Bi-2223 (110 K), Tl-2223 (125 K), Hg-1223 (134 K, the record at ambient pressure). The physics is understood: outer planes are optimally doped by charge reservoirs, while the inner plane is slightly underdoped. For n_L >= 4, inner planes become increasingly underdoped, reducing Tc. This is a genuine physical observation, and the match with n/phi(6)=3 is numerically exact. The physical mechanism (doping penetration depth) is well understood and independent of n=6, but the coincidence is noteworthy.

## H-SC-28: MgB2 Two-Band = phi(6)=2

**Grade: WEAK** (confirmed)

MgB2's two-band superconductivity (sigma and pi bands) arises from boron's sp2 hybridization producing sigma bonds in-plane and pi bonds out-of-plane. The two-band structure is a consequence of boron's electronic configuration, not number theory. Many superconductors are multi-band (iron pnictides have 5 bands crossing the Fermi level), so "2 bands" is not a universal feature. The gap ratio Delta_sigma/Delta_pi ~ 3.2 is loosely n/phi, but this ratio varies with temperature and impurity scattering.

The count of 6 is achievable but requires specific choices about what constitutes a "phase" vs. a "crossover."

## H-SC-35: DC+AC Josephson = 2 Relations

**Grade: CLOSE** (confirmed)

The two Josephson relations -- I = I_c sin(Delta_phi) and V = (hbar/2e)(d(Delta_phi)/dt) -- are the complete description of an ideal Josephson junction. These two equations are universally recognized as the fundamental Josephson equations (see Tinkham Ch. 6, Barone & Paterno). The number 2 = phi(6) matches. While "two equations" is a small number, these are genuinely the complete set, not a subset chosen to match. The 2e factor (Cooper pair charge) ties them together. CLOSE is appropriate.

## H-SC-36: DC SQUID = phi(6)=2 Junctions

**Grade: FAIL** (downgraded from WEAK)

A DC SQUID uses 2 Josephson junctions for quantum interference, analogous to Young's double-slit experiment. The number 2 is the minimum required for interference (you need at least 2 paths). This is not specific to superconductivity -- any interferometer needs at least 2 paths. The RF SQUID uses 1 junction, and multi-junction SQUIDs exist. Calling "2" a match with phi(6) when it is simply the minimum for interference is trivial.

In natural units, K_J would be a different number. Attempting to find n=6 patterns in unit-dependent quantities is methodologically invalid.

## H-SC-40: Nb3Sn Unit Cell: Nb=6, Tc=18, Hc2~24

**Grade: CLOSE** (confirmed)

This is one of the stronger hypotheses because it identifies THREE independent n=6 matches in one material:
1. Unit cell contains 6 Nb atoms (confirmed: A15 structure, 6 Nb + 2 Sn per cell)
2. Tc = 18.3 K ~ 3n = 18 (1.7% off)
3. Hc2(4.2K) ~ 24-30 T, with lower bound ~ J2(6) = 24

The Nb count of 6 is crystallographically exact. The A15 structure has Nb chains along three orthogonal face directions, 2 per face, giving 3*2=6. The Tc and Hc2 matches are approximate but within reasonable range. Three independent matches in one material is statistically more impressive than isolated single matches, though all arise from the specific electronic and lattice properties of the A15 structure.

## H-SC-41: MgB2 Atomic Numbers: Mg Z=12, B Z=5

Mg has Z=12 = sigma(6) and B has Z=5 = sopfr(6). These are exact matches for the atomic numbers. MgB2 is a genuinely important superconductor (highest Tc among conventional BCS superconductors at 39 K, discovered 2001). The coincidence is numerically exact and involves two distinct n=6 functions. However, atomic numbers are fixed properties of elements chosen by nature, and MgB2 being superconducting is determined by its electron-phonon coupling, not by the atomic numbers matching n=6 functions. Still, the double match is notable.

## H-SC-43: SC Qubit Types = 3 Fundamental

**Grade: CLOSE** (confirmed)

The three fundamental superconducting qubit types -- charge, flux, and phase -- correspond to the three conjugate variables of a Josephson junction circuit: charge Q, flux Phi, and phase phi. This classification is standard (Devoret & Schoelkopf, Science 2013; Clarke & Wilhelm, Nature 2008). Modern qubits (transmon, fluxonium, 0-pi qubit) are evolved versions of these three archetypes. The number 3 has a clear physical origin (three energy scales: E_C, E_J, E_L). The match with n/phi(6)=3 is exact, and the classification is robust. CLOSE is appropriate.

## H-SC-45: SC Transition 4 Phenomena = tau(6)

**Grade: CLOSE** (upgraded from CLOSE -- confirmed)

The four hallmark phenomena of the superconducting transition are: (1) zero resistance, (2) Meissner effect, (3) specific heat jump, (4) energy gap formation. These are the standard textbook presentation (Tinkham Ch. 1-3, Ashcroft & Mermin Ch. 34). While additional phenomena occur (thermal conductivity change, ultrasonic absorption change, NMR relaxation rate change), the "big four" are universally cited as the defining experimental signatures. tau(6)=4 matches. This is a well-established pedagogical classification.

## H-SC-46: WHH Coefficient ln(2) = ln(phi(6))

**Grade: CLOSE** (confirmed)

The Werthamer-Helfand-Hohenberg (WHH) formula for the orbital upper critical field gives Hc2(0) = -0.6932 * Tc * (dHc2/dT)|Tc, where 0.6932 = ln(2). This is an exact result from linearizing the Gor'kov equations at H=Hc2. The value ln(2) arises from the digamma function psi(1/2) = -gamma - ln(4) in the pair-breaking theory. The match ln(2) = ln(phi(6)) is mathematically exact. However, ln(2) is one of the most common mathematical constants, appearing in information theory, radioactive decay (half-life), thermodynamics, etc. The connection to phi(6)=2 is formally correct but not uniquely informative.

## H-SC-50: 3 Macroscopic Quantum Effects

**Grade: CLOSE** (confirmed)

The three macroscopic quantum phenomena in superconductors -- flux quantization, Josephson effect, and Meissner effect -- are the standard textbook trio (Tinkham Ch. 3-6, Rose-Innes & Rhoderick). These arise from the macroscopic wavefunction: |Psi|^2 gives the Meissner effect (London equations), while the phase gives flux quantization (single-valuedness) and the Josephson effect (phase difference across a weak link). The classification into 3 is robust and physically grounded. n/phi(6)=3 matches exactly.

## H-SC-52: BCS Gap T-Dependence Coefficient

**Grade: FAIL** (confirmed)

The BCS gap near Tc follows Delta(T)/Delta(0) ~ 1.7408 * sqrt(1-T/Tc). The coefficient 1.7408 = pi/e^gamma (exact), where gamma = 0.5772 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant. The hypothesis notes 1.74 ~ sqrt(3) = 1.732 (0.5% off), but the exact value is pi/e^gamma, which has no connection to n=6. Close numerical matches to irrational numbers are inevitable with enough candidates.

## H-SC-55: SMES Energy Formula 1/2

**Grade: FAIL** (confirmed)

E = (1/2)LI^2 is the energy stored in ANY inductor, superconducting or not. The 1/2 comes from integrating P = LI(dI/dt) from 0 to I, giving (1/2)LI^2. This is freshman electromagnetics. The same factor 1/2 appears in kinetic energy (1/2 mv^2), capacitor energy (1/2 CV^2), and spring energy (1/2 kx^2). It has zero specificity to superconductors.

Three-phase AC power is the universal standard for power transmission, invented by Tesla/Dolivo-Dobrovolsky in the 1880s-1890s. Superconducting cables use 3-phase because they must be compatible with the existing power grid. This has nothing to do with superconductor physics.

## H-SC-58: SC Levitation 3 Mechanisms

**Grade: FAIL** (downgraded from WEAK)

The hypothesis lists 3 levitation mechanisms (Meissner repulsion, flux pinning, eddy current). However, eddy current levitation is not specific to superconductors -- it works with normal conductors too (induction levitation). True superconducting levitation has 2 mechanisms: Meissner repulsion (Type I) and flux pinning (Type II). The "3 stability directions" (vertical, horizontal, rotational) is just the 3 spatial degrees of freedom, which applies to any levitation system. Downgraded for mixing superconductor-specific and generic physics.
