Grading scale:
- **EXACT**: The claimed number/structure matches real-world data precisely, with a legitimate physical basis.
- **CLOSE**: Within ~10% of real values, or directionally correct with a standard counting convention.
- **WEAK**: Requires cherry-picking, flexible counting, or post-hoc rationalization.
- **FAIL**: Contradicted by real-world data, trivially true of any number, unit-dependent, or pure numerology.
- **UNVERIFIABLE**: Insufficient published data to confirm or deny.

## H-FU-1: D-T Nucleon Count 2+3 = 5 = sopfr(6)

**Grade: EXACT (confirmed)**

Deuterium has mass number A=2, tritium has A=3. These are the prime factors of 6. The total nucleon count in the D-T reaction (both reactants and products by conservation) is 2+3=5=sopfr(6). This is arithmetically exact and does not depend on any counting flexibility. The fact that the two lightest fusible isotopes beyond hydrogen have mass numbers matching the prime factorization of 6 is a genuine and striking numerical coincidence. No physical causation -- the mass numbers arise from nuclear stability, not number theory -- but the match is clean and unambiguous. The original EXACT grade is warranted.

## H-FU-2: D-T Energy 17.6 MeV ~ 18

**Grade: WEAK (confirmed)**

The D-T Q-value is 17.6 MeV (precisely 17.589 MeV from AME2020 mass tables). The hypothesis offers three different n=6 expressions all yielding 18: sigma+sopfr+mu, 3n, and J2-n. When you have this many free combinations available and the match is still 2.3% off, the fit is unimpressive. The energy partition (80/20 for neutron/alpha) follows directly from two-body decay kinematics (momentum conservation gives E_alpha/Q = m_n/(m_alpha+m_n) = 1/5), which has nothing to do with n=6. Original WEAK grade is correct.

## H-FU-3: D-D Has phi(6)=2 Branches

**Grade: CLOSE (confirmed)**

D-D fusion does have exactly two branches at comparable probability (~50/50): D+D->T+p and D+D->He3+n. This is well-established experimentally and arises from isospin symmetry. The count of 2 is exact. However, the number 2 is so small and so many n=6 functions yield 2 (phi(6)=2, lambda(6)=2, the prime factor 2) that this is not a discriminating match. The original CLOSE grade is appropriate -- the count is right but not distinctive enough for EXACT.

## H-FU-6: Gamow Peak 1/3 Exponent

**Grade: FAIL (confirmed)**

The 1/3 power in the Gamow peak formula E_peak = (E_G * kT^2 / 4)^(1/3) arises from optimizing the product of a decreasing exponential (Coulomb barrier tunneling) and an increasing exponential (Maxwell-Boltzmann tail). This is standard calculus -- the exponent 1/3 appears whenever you extremize a product of two exponentials whose arguments scale with different powers. Claiming 1/3 relates to 6 having 3 as a divisor is a textbook example of post-hoc rationalization. The original FAIL grade is correct.

## H-FU-7: Conservation Laws = n = 6

**Grade: WEAK (downgrade from CLOSE)**

The hypothesis claims 6 conservation laws in nuclear reactions: energy, momentum, angular momentum, charge, baryon number, lepton number. However, this counts vector quantities (momentum = 3 components, angular momentum = 3 components) as single scalars. In Noether's theorem terms, the Poincare group gives 10 conserved quantities. Additionally, one could add isospin (approximate), parity, or CP. The count of 6 requires a specific convention for grouping that is not universal. Downgraded from CLOSE to WEAK because the counting flexibility is significant -- standard physics texts do not consistently list "6 conservation laws."

## H-FU-60: Solar pp-chain 6 Steps

Downgraded from CLOSE to FAIL because the boundary choice is arbitrary and designed to hit the target.
