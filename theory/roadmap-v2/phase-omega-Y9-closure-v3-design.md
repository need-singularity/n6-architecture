## S9 v3 Successor Design

### 9.1 Ten Lessons from v2

#### Lesson 1: emergence depletion outperforms top-down

v1's top-down axis assignment (PURE/PROBLEM/N6) injected researcher bias into the axis structure. v2's bottom-up emergence depletion (R1~R3) extracts axes from data, reducing bias. As a result, unexpected axes like Y3 (COMPUTATIONAL-TAU) and Y4 (GATE-BARRIER) emerged in R2, and these provided the most honest analyses for BT-542.

#### Lesson 2: meta honesty axis is essential

The existence of Y9 HONEST-HARNESS guaranteed the honesty of all v2. Achieving self-reference 0, MISS concealment 0 across Phases P1~P6 was possible because Y9 operated as a formal gate. v3 must keep the meta honesty axis.

#### Lesson 3: MISS records can be more valuable than EXACT records

v2's 12+ MISS records precisely documented "what does not work". These negative results help future researchers (human or AI) avoid repeating the same mistakes. In v1, MISS existed only informally.

#### Lesson 4: rewriting-vs-demonstration distinction prevents self-deception

The tag stating beta_0 = sigma - sopfr = 7 is "not demonstration but rewriting" may seem minor, but without this distinction it is possible to slide into the overclaim that "n=6 explains Yang-Mills mass gap". 3-way tagging (rewriting/conditional/observation) will be maintained in v3, possibly further subdivided.

#### Lesson 5: assigning 2 BTs to a single Phase is efficient

P4 (BT-543 + BT-544) and P5 (BT-545 + BT-546) dual-attack modes promoted cross-observation (Y5<->Y6, Y7<->Y8). In particular, 4 mappings + 3 tools emerged from the Y5<->Y6 cross. v3 may attempt 3-BT simultaneous assignment.

#### Lesson 6: atlas.n6 actual-edit 0 is both safeguard and bottleneck

Actual-edit 0 by L0 Guard prevented data contamination but also created a bottleneck with 14 drafts queued without being reflected. v3 can introduce a "conditional atlas-edit within Phase" mechanism (confirmed after Y9 audit at Phase close).

#### Lesson 7: utility score is "infrastructure richness", not "resolution possibility"

The high utility of Y1 (9.5) and Y4 (9.4) meant "this axis has rich surrounding tools", not "this axis can resolve the BT". v3 should separate "resolution-possibility estimate" as a distinct metric.

#### Lesson 8: C2 (monotone invariant) lack is a common weakness across all axes

Of the 5 decisive tool features extracted in P6, C2 was "weak" on every axis except Y6. Given Perelman's core technique was monotone W-entropy decrease, v3's top priority should be "searching for monotone invariants matched to each BT".

#### Lesson 9: retrospective Phase has higher cost-effectiveness than attack Phase

P6 (600 lines) is shorter than P2~P5 (avg 1092 lines), but provided meta-level insight in C1~C5 decisive tool features. v3 can insert retrospective Phases in the middle to form "attack-retrospective-attack" cycles.

#### Lesson 10: resolution count 0/6 is not failure but honesty

Of the 7 Millennium problems, only 1 has been resolved in 62 years (Poincaré, 2002-2003). 0/6 reflects the difficulty of these problems, not project failure. v2's value is completing an "honest topography", and this topography serves as the starting ground for v3.
